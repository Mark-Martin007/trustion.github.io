Scammed? Do These Steps First (Checklist)

This checklist helps you act quickly, secure your accounts/devices, and follow proper reporting/dispute steps. If you need remote help now, call +1 844 677 2312.

Important: We provide technical support and guidance. We do not guarantee recovery of funds—outcomes depend on banks, platforms, and official processes.

1) Stop the damage (first 10–30 minutes)

  • If you gave remote access: disconnect the session, turn off Wi‑Fi, and shut down the device if needed.
  • If you shared passwords: change them immediately (start with email + banking).
  • Enable MFA (two‑factor) on email, banking, and social accounts.
  • Check account recovery settings (backup email/phone) and remove anything unfamiliar.

2) Contact your bank/card/platform

  • Call your bank/card issuer and report fraud.
  • Ask about dispute/chargeback options and timelines.
  • If it was a wire/transfer, report it immediately (time matters).
  • If it was crypto, report to the exchange/platform and preserve wallet/transaction IDs.

3) Preserve evidence (don’t delete)

  • Save screenshots of chats, emails, and webpages.
  • Record transaction IDs, dates/times, amounts, and recipient details.
  • Keep phone numbers, email addresses, wallet IDs, and any “support ticket” numbers.
  • Write a short timeline: what happened, when, and what you shared/installed.

4) Clean and secure your device

  • Remove suspicious programs and browser extensions.
  • Run reputable malware scans and verify system settings.
  • Update OS and apps; patch the browser.
  • Check startup apps, remote access tools, and admin accounts.

5) Watch for follow‑up scams

  • Be cautious of “recovery agents” who promise guaranteed refunds.
  • Don’t pay upfront fees to “unlock” your money.
  • Verify official numbers and URLs (don’t trust links in emails/texts).
  • Expect scammers to try again once they know you’re a target.
If you’re unsure: call Trustion and we’ll guide you through safe next steps remotely.

What to have ready when you call

  • Device type (Windows/macOS) and what you’re seeing
  • Approx. time/date of the incident
  • Transaction reference(s), platform used, and any chat/email proof
