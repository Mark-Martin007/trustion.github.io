Scam Help Checklist

If you think you’ve been scammed, don’t panic—follow these exact steps to limit the damage and boost your chance of recovery. Need step-by-step remote help? Call: +1 844 677 2312
We provide technical and evidence support. We do not guarantee money recovery—results depend on banks/platforms and reporting quickly. Read this checklist even if you have already taken action!

1) Stop Further Damage 🛑

  • Close your browser and any remote desktop apps (like AnyDesk, TeamViewer, etc).
  • Shut down internet if a criminal is still connected.
  • Immediately change passwords on email, banking, and key accounts from another clean device if possible.
  • Turn on 2FA/MFA on major accounts.

2) Contact Banks & Platforms 💳

  • Call your bank/credit card company and report the scam—ask about disputes and freezing transactions.
  • If wire or crypto: contact the platform or exchange urgently. Copy all transaction IDs now.
  • Use only official phone numbers/websites (never links sent by scammers).

3) Gather & Save Evidence 📁

  • Screenshot all relevant emails, chats, or receipts.
  • Note exact times, sums, transaction numbers, the criminal's details (phone/email/wallet), and what you shared.
  • Do not delete communication—save it.

4) Secure Your Devices 🔒

  • Run a full malware/virus scan.
  • Remove any unusual apps or browser extensions.
  • Update your operating system and all apps.
  • If in doubt—get professional remote help.

5) Watch for Follow-Up Scams 🚨

  • Don’t pay “guaranteed recovery agents” who ask for money upfront.
  • Be skeptical of calls/emails claiming to be law enforcement or “refund” offers.
  • Verify any number using the institution’s real website (never from the scammer’s message).
